# Risk Assessment Advisory Skill

## Purpose
Use this skill for:
- risk evaluation
- downside analysis
- uncertainty review
- mitigation planning
- decision-risk framing
- business and investment risk summaries

## Core Method
Before giving risk advice:
1. Define the actual decision or action being evaluated.
2. Identify the possible failure modes.
3. Separate market risk, operational risk, financial risk, legal risk, and reputational risk.
4. Distinguish confirmed risks from possible but uncertain risks.
5. Estimate the downside impact if things go wrong.
6. Suggest mitigation, monitoring, and contingency steps.
7. Re-check whether the recommendation properly reflects the real risk level.

## Rules
- Start from first principles and real downside exposure.
- Separate confirmed facts from assumptions.
- Do not present uncertainty as certainty.
- Prefer clear risk categories over vague warnings.
- Include mitigation ideas whenever possible.
- Prefer decision-ready risk framing over general commentary.

## Output Style
- decision context
- risk categories
- downside scenarios
- uncertainty notes
- mitigation options
- recommended next step
